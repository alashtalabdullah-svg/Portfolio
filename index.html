About Me

My beginnings were in coordinating operations between the CEO and the team in the import sector. Part of my job was finding international partners, corresponding with companies, and following up on daily work details with the team. Amidst the busy workload and management's schedule, I took on greater operational responsibilities and became the support system that reported updates and aided in decision-making.

Following this period, I gained greater trust from management and participated in founding an operational project from scratch. I manually established the schedules and systems to control expenses and inventory before digitizing the process.

Trust grew further, and I took over direct management of the project, becoming a team leader for 10 people. My entire focus was on controlling costs, reducing waste, organizing responsibilities, and strengthening internal control and quality assurance.

Naturally, I lean towards firmness when I see chaos, but at the same time, I make sure to understand the reasons clearly before making any decision. Today, I have moved to the Saudi market to contribute to building an organization looking for someone to help achieve great accomplishments.

How I Think?

When an operational problem arises, I don't look at the surface; I search for the root cause. I rely on field observation first, then review the numbers to get the complete picture.

I like to define everyone's responsibilities clearly to avoid work overlap, and I set up a simple tracking system we can measure daily. I believe control is never about complication... control means clarity. A clear administrative structure reduces errors and prevents their recurrence.
